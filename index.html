ChromeLens

Seeing your site from the eyes of the vision impaired

What is ChromeLens

More than 280 million people worldwide are visually impaired. That means that 1 in 26 people who surf your website will not be able to see it as you do. ChromeLens is a set of developer tools that allow developers to code websites better suited for the visually impaired.

Color Blindess

Browsing the internet is something that is natural and easy to us, but to many people who suffer from vision disabilities and problems, it can be a challenging, time consuming and frustrating process.

ChromeLens

To help developers make their web pages friendly to the visually impaired, we created ChromeLens, an extension to Chrome that changes the look of web pages to reflect what they see.

ChromeLens Filters

By checking the 'Enable Lens' checkbox, you turn on the color filters for ChromeLens, letting you choose between various 'color blindness' options, such as Protanopia which is restricted vision of the color red..

ChromeLens Scanner

The ChromeLens Scanner will scan your webpage and assess how well it is designed for people with vision impairment. It will highlight areas for improvement and provide suggestions on how you can improve upon your design.

ChromeLens Tracker

ChromeLens Tracker is a blindness simulatior that shows tha pathway by which a blind user will go through when browsing your web page with a screen reader. You can check if your web page goes through a logical flow and that the important content is included for the screen reader to narrate..
